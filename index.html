About me

I'm a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Mechanical Engineering at Stanford University advised by Dr. John Dabiri. As a researcher, I am interested in using experiments in fluid mechanics to support renewable energy technologies. My current work is centered around using videos of fluid structure interactions to predict wind speeds, with potential applications in resource quantification for distributed wind energy. Outside of research, I am on the leadership team of the Stanford Mechanical Engineering Women's Group, which aims to foster community amongst women graduate students in engineering. In my free time I am an avid hiker, old time fiddler, and amateur hair dresser.
